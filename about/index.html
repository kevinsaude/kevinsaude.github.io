About Me

I am currently pursuing a PhD in political science at the University of Limerick, Ireland. Supervised by Dr. Rory Costello, my dissertation focuses on political identities in Western Europe throughout the 2008-2014 crises. I am also a Teaching Assistant in comparative European politics, and Politics of the European Union.

PhD dissertation (completion expected in June 2022)

Title:

The causal effect of party system polarisation on positive and negative partisanship between 1999-2020 in Western Europe

Abstract:

Are party attachments progressively being replaced with disdain? Previous work has shown that polarisation causes the strength of party attachments to increase, but little is known on negative partisanship. Recent contributions suggest that negative feelings are part and parcel of electoral politics, and that this negativity seemingly increased throughout the 2008-2014 crises in the EU. My research will further our knowledge on the link between polarisation and partisanship in the following avenues:

  • Measure of polarisation on LR and sub-dimensions
  • Focus on Negative Partisanship
  • Fine-grained data in single-country analyses
